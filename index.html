Dr. Cheryl Seals is an associate professor in Auburn University's Department of Computer Science and Software Engineering.

Dr. Seal's research areas of expertise consists of human computer interaction, user interface design, usability evaluation and educational gaming technologies. Seals also works with outreach initiatives to improve computer science education at all levels.

The programs are focused on increasing the computing pipeline by getting students interested in STEM disciplines and future technology careers.

Courses

Comp 7970: Game Design and Development

This course provides an intros to current/future techniques for game design and programming. Topics will include graphics, game engines, motion generation, autonomous character behavior control, interaction structure, and social and interface issues of multi-user play.


Comp 7620: Human Computer Interaction

Theory and practice of human computer interaction; designing, implementing and evaluating human computer interation.

Comp 6620: User Interface Design and Evaluation

Theory and practice of designing interfaces for interactive systems, usability engineering techniques; implementing and evaluating interfaces.

Comp 7930: Machine Intelligence

This course will provide a broad overview of existing methods for machine learning and an introduction to self-organizing systems in general. By the end of the semester you should be familiar with the central concepts and most commonly used methods in this field.

Comp 4640: Intelligent and Interactive Systems

This course combines classical Artificial Intelligence and Interactive Systems. Some of the topics addressed are intelligent behavior in artifacts, perception, reasoning, learning, and acting in complex environments. Interactive Systems will explore systems that are intelligent and the interaction between humans and computers.

Comp 4730 : Computer Ethics

Computer Ethics provides students a foundation to understand the application of ethical principles to practical problems encountered by the practicing computer professional.

Gaming Concepts & Technology


Education has taken many hits with reduction of national educational budgets. We want to explore gaming technology as a way to have kids more interested in education. Kids will play video games for hours and hours because it is fun, we want to create educational applications that are fun so that as a side effect children learn more about subjects that are of interest to National Educators.

SimBuilder Science Project


Project for 4th and 5th grade science where students will learn to create educational simulations with direct manipulation and visual programming techniques.

AU Computer Science Survey Maker


This project is to faciltate empirical research by supporting the online creation of surveys and support the collection and storing of data online with the capability to store surveys long term.

The KEMET Project


Knowledge by Education Math Engineering and Technology This will be a short 2-4 week summer program that will teach Engineering, Math & Computer Technology information to 6 & 7th grade students with quarterly outreach activities.

The Global Bridges Project


Educational software to support project in Beijing China that currently teaches chinese pre-k children english. This project is sponsored by the department of Education and we can partner with them to create software to support this effort.

Auburn Human Computer Interaction Lab


The HCI Lab provides users unique interactions with technology gives us a unique perspective on designing more robust systems. The systems that we create & study are in the areas of Mobile Phones, Cloud Computing, and Educational Games to support teaching in STEM areas and programming.
